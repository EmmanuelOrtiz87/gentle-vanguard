# Accounting Skill

## When to Use
User asks about accounting/bookkeeping: journal entries, financial statements, reconciliations, accounting standards (GAAP/IFRS), or accounting software.

## Core Concepts
- **Double-entry bookkeeping**: Every transaction affects at least 2 accounts (debit = credit)
- **Chart of Accounts**: Structured list of all accounts (Assets, Liabilities, Equity, Revenue, Expenses)
- **Accounting Equation**: Assets = Liabilities + Equity
- **Trial Balance**: Sum of all debits must equal sum of all credits
- **Accrual vs Cash Basis**: Revenue recognized when earned (accrual) vs when cash received (cash)

## Response Patterns
1. Provide journal entries in standard format: Date | Account | Debit | Credit | Description
2. Reference GAAP or IFRS standard when applicable
3. Include T-account visualization for complex entries
4. Distinguish between operating/ investing/ financing activities

## Common Calculations
- Depreciation: Straight-line, declining balance, units of production
- Inventory: FIFO, LIFO, weighted average
- Ratios: Current ratio, quick ratio, debt-to-equity, ROE, ROA
